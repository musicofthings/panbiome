Part One

South Indian Panbiome Recipes

Fermented batters, spiced lentils, and ancient grains — South Indian cuisine is one of the world's oldest microbiome-nourishing traditions.

1
Serves 4 35 min
Soft Idli with Coconut Chutney & Drumstick Sambar
🦠Natural fermentation creates probiotics; drumstick & vegetables provide prebiotic fibre.
Ingredients
  • Idli batter (rice + urad dal)
  • Fresh coconut, green chillies, curry leaves
  • Toor dal, drumstick, tomato, spinach
  • Tamarind, sambar powder
Steps
  1. Steam idlis 12 min.
  2. Blend coconut chutney with chillies and curry leaves.
  3. Simmer sambar with vegetables 20 min.
💡 Use millet idli batter for extra fibre.
2
Serves 4 20 min
Ragi Upma with Peas & Curry Leaves
🦠Ragi is high in resistant starch that feeds butyrate-producing bacteria.
Ingredients
  • Ragi flour
  • Peas, tomato, curry leaves
  • Mustard seeds, turmeric
Steps
  1. Roast ragi flour until fragrant.
  2. Temper mustard seeds, sauté vegetables.
  3. Cook like upma — add water gradually, stir well.
💡 Add grated carrot for colour and extra plants.
3
Serves 4 25 min
Methi Sabzi with Bajra Roti
🦠Fenugreek leaves are prebiotic and anti-inflammatory.
Ingredients
  • Fresh methi (fenugreek) leaves
  • Onion, tomato, spices
  • Bajra (pearl millet) flour
Steps
  1. Stir-fry methi sabzi with onion, tomato, and spices.
  2. Cook bajra rotis on a hot tawa.
💡 Swap methi with spinach or gongura for variety.
4
Serves 4 20 min
Banana Stem Thoran
🦠High insoluble fibre improves gut motility.
Ingredients
  • Banana stem, grated coconut
  • Curry leaves, mustard seeds
  • Turmeric, salt
Steps
  1. Chop banana stem finely (remove fibrous strands).
  2. Stir-fry with mustard and curry leaves.
  3. Finish with grated coconut.
💡 Banana stem is common in South Indian markets — ask for vazhai thandu.
5
Serves 4 25 min
Mixed Dal Sambar with Gongura Leaves
🦠Multiple dals + sour greens create diverse prebiotics.
Ingredients
  • Mung + masoor dal
  • Gongura (sorrel) leaves, mixed vegetables
  • Sambar powder, tamarind
Steps
  1. Pressure-cook dals until soft.
  2. Add gongura, vegetables, and sambar powder.
  3. Temper with mustard seeds and curry leaves.
💡 Gongura adds tangy probiotic support — it's a sour-leaf staple of Andhra kitchens.
6
Serves 4 10 min
Sprouted Mung Bean Salad
🦠Sprouting increases bioavailability and live enzymes.
Ingredients
  • Sprouted mung beans
  • Cucumber, tomato, coriander
  • Lemon juice, salt
Steps
  1. Toss all ingredients raw.
  2. Squeeze lemon and serve immediately.
💡 Sprout at home — soak overnight, rinse twice daily for 2 days.
7
Serves 4 10 min
Curd Rice with Roasted Jeera
🦠Probiotic curd + resistant starch from cooled rice.
Ingredients
  • Cooled cooked rice
  • Fresh curd (yogurt)
  • Roasted cumin, curry leaves, salt
Steps
  1. Mix cooled rice with curd until creamy.
  2. Temper mustard and curry leaves, stir in.
  3. Top with roasted cumin.
💡 Use brown rice for extra fibre. Cooling rice overnight doubles its resistant starch.
8
Serves 4 25 min
Adai (Mixed Lentil Dosa)
🦠Multiple dals provide complete protein and prebiotic fibre.
Ingredients
  • Rice, mixed dals (chana, toor, urad, masoor)
  • Dried red chillies, spices, curry leaves
Steps
  1. Soak rice and dals together 4–6 hours.
  2. Grind coarsely with spices.
  3. Make thick dosas on a hot tawa.
💡 Ferment overnight for extra probiotics — batter becomes slightly sour and more gut-friendly.

Part Two

Mediterranean Panbiome Recipes

Extra-virgin olive oil, legumes, and wild herbs — the Mediterranean diet is the most studied diet for microbiome diversity and longevity.

9
Serves 4 10 min
Greek Yogurt Bowl with Olive Oil, Herbs & Chickpeas
🦠EVOO polyphenols feed beneficial bacteria; chickpeas add prebiotic fibre.
Ingredients
  • Thick Greek yogurt
  • Extra-virgin olive oil, chickpeas
  • Fresh dill or mint, garlic, lemon
Steps
  1. Mix yogurt with herbs and crushed garlic.
  2. Top with chickpeas.
  3. Drizzle generously with olive oil.
💡 Use as a dip or side dish alongside your Indian meal for a cross-cultural Panbiome plate.
10
Serves 4 35 min
Lentil & Vegetable Stew
🦠High fibre from lentils + vegetables; olive oil polyphenols.
Ingredients
  • Brown lentils
  • Eggplant, zucchini, tomato, onion
  • Extra-virgin olive oil, dried oregano
Steps
  1. Sauté onion and vegetables in olive oil.
  2. Add lentils and water, simmer 25 min.
  3. Finish with a generous drizzle of fresh olive oil.
💡 Serve with whole-grain bread. The second drizzle of raw EVOO at the end preserves its polyphenols.
11
Serves 4 10 min
Chickpea Salad with Olive Oil Dressing
🦠Legumes + raw veggies increase microbial diversity.
Ingredients
  • Chickpeas (cooked or canned)
  • Cucumber, tomato, red onion, parsley
  • Extra-virgin olive oil, lemon juice
Steps
  1. Toss all ingredients together raw.
  2. Dress with olive oil and fresh lemon.
💡 Add olives for extra polyphenols. This is a complete Panbiome meal in a bowl.
12
Serves 4 30 min
Baked Falafel Bowl with Tahini-Yogurt Sauce
🦠Chickpea-based, baked (not fried), served with probiotic yogurt.
Ingredients
  • Chickpeas, fresh parsley and coriander
  • Cumin, garlic, tahini, yogurt, lemon
Steps
  1. Blend chickpeas with herbs and spices.
  2. Shape into patties, bake at 200°C for 20 min.
  3. Mix tahini and yogurt for sauce.
💡 Use as a wrap filling or bowl topping over brown rice or whole-grain pitta.
13
Serves 4 30 min
Roasted Vegetable Plate with Olive Oil
🦠Polyphenols from olive oil + diverse roasted plants.
Ingredients
  • Bell peppers, carrot, broccoli, cauliflower
  • Whole garlic cloves, extra-virgin olive oil
Steps
  1. Toss vegetables with olive oil and garlic.
  2. Roast at 200°C for 25 min.
  3. Finish with fresh olive oil drizzle before serving.
💡 Add whole garlic cloves — roasted garlic is a powerful prebiotic and becomes sweet and mild.
14
Serves 4 25 min
White Bean & Spinach Stew
🦠Beans + greens provide prebiotics and polyphenols.
Ingredients
  • White beans (cannellini)
  • Spinach, garlic, tomato, fresh herbs
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
Steps
  1. Sauté garlic, add tomato, beans, and water.
  2. Simmer 20 min, stir in spinach and herbs.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil.
💡 Add a squeeze of lemon before serving to brighten the flavour and boost iron absorption.
15
Serves 4 35 min
Eggplant & Tomato Bake with Olive Oil
🦠Eggplant fibre + tomato lycopene + olive oil polyphenols.
Ingredients
  • Eggplant, tomato, onion
  • Extra-virgin olive oil, oregano, garlic
Steps
  1. Layer sliced vegetables in a baking dish.
  2. Drizzle generously with olive oil.
  3. Bake at 180°C for 30 min.
💡 Serve with a spoonful of yogurt on top — adding probiotic to every meal is a Panbiome habit.
16
Serves 4 30 min
Red Lentil Soup with Olive Oil Drizzle
🦠Lentils + olive oil create a prebiotic-rich, satiating meal.
Ingredients
  • Red lentils, carrot, onion, garlic
  • Cumin, spinach, extra-virgin olive oil
Steps
  1. Sauté aromatics, add lentils and water.
  2. Simmer 25 min, blend partially.
  3. Add spinach; finish with olive oil drizzle.
💡 Add a pinch of smoked paprika for depth. The raw olive oil drizzle added after cooking preserves its microbiome benefits.

Part Three

Okinawan Panbiome Recipes

Seaweed, fermented miso, and longevity vegetables — Okinawa's Blue Zone diet is extraordinary for gut health, particularly Akkermansia muciniphila.

17
Serves 4 15 min
Wakame Miso Soup
🦠Fucoidan in wakame feeds Akkermansia muciniphila; miso is a fermented probiotic food.
Ingredients
  • Dried wakame seaweed
  • Miso paste (white or red), silken tofu
  • Green onion, kombu or vegetable stock
Steps
  1. Soak wakame 5 min, drain and chop.
  2. Heat stock gently; add tofu and wakame.
  3. Remove from heat; dissolve miso in a ladle of soup, stir in.
  4. Top with green onion.
💡 Never boil after adding miso — heat kills the probiotic cultures.
18
Serves 4 20 min
Goya Champuru (Bitter Melon Stir-Fry)
🦠Bitter melon supports blood-sugar balance and feeds beneficial gut bacteria.
Ingredients
  • Bitter melon (karela)
  • Firm tofu, carrot, onion
  • Turmeric, light soy sauce
Steps
  1. Slice bitter melon; soak in salted water 10 min, drain.
  2. Press and cube tofu.
  3. Stir-fry onion and carrot 3 min, add tofu and bitter melon, cook 8 min.
  4. Season with turmeric and soy sauce.
💡 Karela (bitter melon) is common in South Indian kitchens — this dish bridges both traditions beautifully.
19
Serves 4 25 min
Purple Sweet Potato Mash with Nori Flakes
🦠Resistant starch + anthocyanins + seaweed prebiotic fibre from nori.
Ingredients
  • Purple sweet potatoes
  • Dried nori flakes
  • A little olive oil, salt
Steps
  1. Boil sweet potatoes until tender, drain.
  2. Mash with a drizzle of olive oil and salt.
  3. Sprinkle generously with nori flakes.
💡 Orange sweet potatoes work if purple is unavailable — same prebiotic benefit, fewer anthocyanins.
20
Serves 4 20 min
Kombu Vegetable Clear Soup
🦠Kombu polysaccharides are powerful prebiotics; mineral-rich and deeply nourishing.
Ingredients
  • Dried kombu
  • Daikon or carrot, spinach, mushrooms
  • Light miso or salt
Steps
  1. Soak kombu 10 min; bring to gentle simmer, remove before boiling.
  2. Add sliced vegetables and mushrooms; simmer 10 min.
  3. Season with miso or salt. Add spinach last.
💡 Save the soaked kombu — slice thin and add to rice or stir-fries for extra prebiotic benefit.
21
Serves 4 10 min
Seaweed Salad (Mozuku or Nori)
🦠One of the richest natural sources of fucoidan — feeds Akkermansia muciniphila directly.
Ingredients
  • Rehydrated mozuku or nori sheets
  • Cucumber, light rice vinegar
  • Sesame seeds
Steps
  1. Rehydrate dried seaweed in cold water 5 min, drain.
  2. Slice cucumber thin.
  3. Toss with vinegar and sesame seeds.
💡 Serve as a refreshing side dish — a tablespoon of seaweed a day is a Panbiome habit worth keeping.
22
Serves 4 20 min
Sweet Potato & Tofu Stir-Fry with Seaweed Garnish
🦠Resistant starch + plant protein + seaweed prebiotic topping.
Ingredients
  • Sweet potato cubes, firm tofu
  • Mixed greens, turmeric
  • Dried seaweed flakes
Steps
  1. Press and cube tofu.
  2. Stir-fry sweet potato until golden, add tofu and greens.
  3. Season with turmeric. Plate and top with seaweed flakes.
💡 Add sliced bitter melon for extra Okinawan authenticity — and extra Panbiome benefit.
23
Serves 4 25 min
Miso-Glazed Roasted Vegetables with Seaweed
🦠Fermented miso glaze + seaweed prebiotics on diverse roasted plants.
Ingredients
  • Mixed vegetables (broccoli, carrot, sweet potato, pumpkin)
  • Miso paste, a little olive oil
  • Dried seaweed flakes
Steps
  1. Toss vegetables with olive oil; roast at 200°C for 20 min.
  2. Mix miso paste with warm water to make glaze.
  3. Brush on in the last 5 min of roasting.
  4. Sprinkle with seaweed flakes before serving.
💡 Use sweet potato as the base for extra resistant starch — the Okinawan longevity staple.
24
Serves 4 20 min
Hijiki Seaweed & Carrot Stir-Fry
🦠Hijiki provides minerals, iron, and prebiotic fibre; carrot adds beta-carotene.
Ingredients
  • Dried hijiki seaweed
  • Carrot (julienned), firm tofu
  • Light soy sauce, sesame oil
Steps
  1. Soak hijiki 15 min, drain.
  2. Stir-fry carrot 3 min; add tofu and hijiki.
  3. Season with soy sauce and sesame oil; cook 5 min.
💡 This classic Okinawan side pairs beautifully with miso soup and brown rice — a complete Panbiome meal.

How to Use This Recipe Companion

Simple habits, compounded over weeks, transform your inner rainforest.

🌿

Weekly Goal

Pick 2–3 recipes from each cuisine section to easily hit 30+ plants per week.

🔀

Mix & Match

Add South Indian spices to Mediterranean beans, or sprinkle Okinawan seaweed flakes on dal. Cross-tradition combinations are Panbiome gold.

🫒

Mediterranean Rule

Always finish Mediterranean dishes with a drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil added raw after cooking.

🌊

Okinawan Rule

Always finish Okinawan dishes with seaweed flakes or miso — and never boil after adding miso.

Whether your rainforest grows with sambar, olive oil, or seaweed —
the rules are the same: diversity, fibre, love, and respect.
